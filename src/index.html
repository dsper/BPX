Frequently Asked Questions

Why do we need the BPX?

The BPX...

  • drives Partner relationships to operate more efficiently and at scale
  • fills a capability gap, enhances a current offering, and promotes new solutions
  • reduces overlap, introduces efficiencies, and increases revenue

How does the BPX meet these needs?

The BPX functions as a database, search engine, and relationship manager to centralize information sharing and the Partner validation process.

Is training available for new BPX users?

Yes! When registering as a new user, the intake process will conclude with a brief walkthrough tutorial that features navigation tips, key tools, and other features. Additional learning opportunities are available in Degreed as a Learning Pathway.

Can I invite a Booz Allen Partner I've worked with on a project in the past?

Absolutely. All Partner members will go through a standard intake process approved by Ethics, Legal, and Compliance. The BPX is stronger and more efficient with an active base of both Booz Allen employees and In-Network Partners.

Can I join more than one community in the BPX?

Yes. You can join as many communities as you wish. Simply request to join and the community manager will approve your membership.

What documents, agreements, and other legal definitions do I need to put in place as a Partner member?

Part of the Partner intake process includes an NDA. Once you are an approved In-Network Partner member, you will have access to teaming agreements, IP agreements, or additional NDAs as needed.

How are these relationships governed?

A major goal of the BPX is to streamline workflows to increase efficiencies. Legal, Compliance, and Alliances teams and resources will be available to Booz Allen employees to help guide relationships, provide necessary documents and agreements, and answer questions. The overall BPX is governed by an advisory board.
